Open source robotics challenges

By Design and Web Team · Jun 18, 2026

How open source robotics teams turn repeatable delivery into daily product work.

Robotics 12

A content card is a teaser for an article or resource: an image, a clamped heading, author and date, a footer of chips and metadata, and an overlay link that makes the whole card clickable. A ow-content-card-wrapper handles grid spanning, while a column modifier (ow-content-card-cols-2 through -8) sets the card's layout. Add ow-has-image and ow-has-desc to enable the image and the hover description.

Anatomy

The card is built from an ow-content-card-overlay-link, an optional ow-content-card-image-wrapper, and an ow-content-card-content region split into a body (ow-content-card-primary-content plus the sliding ow-content-card-hover-content) and an ow-content-card-footer-container.

Column spans

The wrapper class controls how many grid columns the card occupies and the matching ow-content-card-cols-* sets the internal layout. Use -wrapper-2 with -cols-2 for a vertical card, -wrapper-4/-cols-4 or -wrapper-6/-cols-6 for horizontal cards, and -wrapper-8/-cols-8 for a full-width split. Below is a four-column horizontal card; on wide screens the image sits beside the text.
