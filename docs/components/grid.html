The grid lays a page out as a stack of rows — ow-grid-row — whose direct children are columns. The track count grows with the viewport: 4 columns on small and medium screens, 8 on large. A ow-grid-col-* class only claims its span within the breakpoint it names; outside that range the column falls back to a full-width span and stacks. The split shortcuts and the fluid-breakout layout build on the same grid. Cells below are tinted only to make the tracks visible.

Columns and breakpoints

Name a span per breakpoint with ow-grid-col-{n} (large, 1–8), ow-grid-col-medium-{n} (1–4) and ow-grid-col-small-{n} (1–4). Combine the three on one column to control it at every size; omit a breakpoint to let the column stack full-width there.

Half-ish
Half-ish
Markup
<div class="ow-grid-row">
  <div class="ow-grid-col-4 ow-grid-col-medium-2 ow-grid-col-small-2">
    Half-ish
  </div>
  <div class="ow-grid-col-4 ow-grid-col-medium-2 ow-grid-col-small-2">
    Half-ish
  </div>
</div>

Split shortcuts

Apply a split class to the row — ow-grid-row-50-50, ow-grid-row-25-75, ow-grid-row-75-25, ow-grid-row-25-25-50 or ow-grid-row-25-25-25-25 — and its ow-grid-col children arrange themselves without per-column classes. Widths are always multiples of 25% of the row.

25%
25%
50%
Markup
<div class="ow-grid-row-25-25-50">
  <div class="ow-grid-col">25%</div>
  <div class="ow-grid-col">25%</div>
  <div class="ow-grid-col">50%</div>
</div>

Offset and order

Pin a column to a track with ow-grid-col-start-large-{n} and reorder it with ow-grid-col-order-large-{n} (with -medium-/-small- variants). Both reset at the next breakpoint, so an offset on large leaves the smaller sizes untouched.

Starts at track 4, drawn last
Drawn first
Markup
<div class="ow-grid-row">
  <div class="ow-grid-col-4 ow-grid-col-start-large-4 ow-grid-col-order-large-2">
    Starts at track 4, drawn last
  </div>
  <div class="ow-grid-col-3 ow-grid-col-order-large-1">
    Drawn first
  </div>
</div>

Nested columns

A ow-grid-row nested inside a column re-divides only that column's width, dropping the page margins and width cap so it fills its parent.

.ow-grid-col-8
.ow-grid-col-6
.ow-grid-col-2
Markup
<div class="ow-grid-row">
  <div class="ow-grid-col-8">
    .ow-grid-col-8
    <div class="ow-grid-row">
      <div class="ow-grid-col-6">.ow-grid-col-6</div>
      <div class="ow-grid-col-2">.ow-grid-col-2</div>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

Fixed-width container

ow-fixed-width constrains content to the grid's width and margins without becoming a grid itself — handy for prose or a single centred column that should line up with the rest of the page.

Centred and capped at the grid's max-width.
Markup
<div class="ow-fixed-width">
  Centred and capped at the grid's max-width.
</div>

Fluid breakout

The fluid-breakout layout lets dense content bleed past the grid's margins. A ow-fluid-breakout wrapper is a single padded column below the large breakpoint; above it, it becomes a capped centre area with a flexible ow-fluid-breakout-aside on either side. The ow-fluid-breakout-main area auto-fills equal items across the page, fitting more than the regular grid would allow.

  <div class="ow-fluid-breakout-main">
    <article class="ow-fluid-breakout-item">Card 1</article>
    <article class="ow-fluid-breakout-item">Card 2</article>
    <article class="ow-fluid-breakout-item">Card 3</article>
  </div>
</div>

Full-width breakout

With no aside, add is-full-width to the main area so it spans all three columns — useful for a wide table or chart that would otherwise be truncated.
